Language: C/C++ (ino)
Peripherals: SPI
Hardware: Arduino Nano, LoRa, Thermistor, LEDs

This project was an exhibition of my studies at the University of Oklahoma. The engineering department accepts requests from external entities to design and engineer products they need. Here my team and I designed an intelligent embedded highway reflector for a retired Highway and Safety Administration civil engineer. The design was to create a more diverse and foward thinking product that could help drivers in various conditions. This version of a lane reflector would feature internal LED's to light up the lanes to increase visibility during heavy rains. The lane reflector also collects temperature data for the portion of the road it is in and reports that data to a host via LoRa. There the data would be collected for statistics and alert drivers the road could be frozen. Eventually, this data would just go straight to the smart vehicles on the road.
